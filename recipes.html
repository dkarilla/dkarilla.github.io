Brazilian Recipes

Feijoada

Rice and beans is a staple in the Brazilian diet. Brazilians are also meat-eaters. Feijoada is a dish the combines the two. Usually with black beans, feijoada follows the basic steps of cooking black beans, but with various meat parts and sausages boiled with the beans. In this video, my mother describes when feijoada is and the basic process of cooking it.

Ingredients:

  • One package of black beans
  • Sausage
  • Pork feet
  • Pork tail
  • One cup of water for every cup of beans
  • Salt or other desired seasonings

Directions:

  • Salt and season meats overnight
  • Wash and soak the beans for four hours
  • Pour beans and water into pressure cooker
  • Place meat on top
  • Boil for at least an hour on medium-high
  • Break up meat
  • Serve over white rice

Påo de Queijo

Påo de Queijo is a simple biscuit made from yuca or manioc flour. In the U.S., this type of flour is called tapioca flour. In this video, my mother goes through the process of making this biscuits, which are often considered a Brazilian breakfast food.

Ingredients:

  • Half a cup of water
  • Half a cup of oil
  • Three eggs
  • A cup of grated cheese
  • One package of tapioca flour

Directions:

  • Boil water and oil together
  • Pour water and oil slowly over tapioca flour and mix
  • Add cheese and mix
  • Beat eggs together
  • Add eggs and mix very well until dough forms
  • Roll dough into balls and place at least two inches apart
  • Bake on 350 degrees for 20 minutes
